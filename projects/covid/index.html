Our Covid-19 Detection System

Covid-19 Chest X-Ray Detection System

Our purpose it to provide a testing solution to the ongoing Covid-19 pandemic. We want to provide quick and accurate detection of Covid through lung x-rays to areas that lack the available medical personale to test their surronding population.

Our Application

Our Automated Covid-19 Detection System is a website application that we have made free and available to all.

Our application can process images of chest rays from multiple different patients instantaneously to determine if the patient is healthy, has pneumonia, or has covid infected lungs.

This application is a spin-off of our team's Automated Malaria Early Detection System.

Motivation

Our team is motivated to make an impact on the world and its current experience with the global pandemic with our application where Covid-19 can be quickly diagnosed and more accessible. With this application, we intend to reduce the time overhead with Covid diagnosis and therefore give back precious, life-saving time to the medical professionals

Design and Deliverables

To achieve our application, we used the following technologies:

Machine Learning

We trained a two-tier machine learning model for our application. It can identify between three categories: healthy, pneumonia, and covid-19 lung x-rays.

Computer Vision

Using computer vision, our model takes in lung x-ray images as input. Our application can make decisions through image recognition.

Website Application

Accessible through the internet, hospitals around the world do not need to invest in specific devices to gain access to this service.

Excel Compatibility

For ease of data scientists and researchers, we created a feature to allow our application's outputs and decisions to be exported to an excel sheet.

Real-life Updates

As more data and research on Covid-19 is released, we retrain and update the model to reflect leading covid-19 researchers.

Technical Growth and Challenges

Pivoting Diseases

Our first application was a Malaria detection application. We decided that the pandemic Covid-19 was a new, big-priority space to explore creating a diagnosis tool with AI computer vision.

Our Machine Learning team spent a lot of time reading through Covid-19 research papers to determine that doctors can use lung x-rays to detect covid-specific pneumonia.

Multi-Layer ML Model

After a test with a golden gate bridge picture marked hilariously as covid, we realise that we would need several layers to shift through images and create multiple categorizations.

The first layer is a pneumonia classifier and the second is a covid-specific pneumonia classifier

Designing for the User

Discussing common ailments of research with some graduate school friends, we learned that it's common to go through immense amounts of data daily.

User-centric design is important and we want our application to be impactful, so we created an easy image-name-to-diagnosis mapping and included an export features for Excel tables.
